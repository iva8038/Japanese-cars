Iconic JDM Sports Cars

Nissan Skyline GT-R (R32, R33, R34)

The Skyline GT-R earned the nickname “Godzilla” for its monstrous performance on the track. With its advanced AWD system and the legendary RB26DETT twin-turbo engine, the R32, R33, and R34 models dominated Japanese racing.


More

Toyota Supra (A80 / Mk4)

The Mk4 Supra is powered by the legendary 2JZ-GTE engine, capable of reaching over 1000 HP with modifications. Known for its tunability and sleek design, it became an icon in the tuning world.


More

Mazda RX-7 (FD3S)

The RX-7 is a lightweight, balanced sports car with a rotary engine that delivers high-revving excitement. Its unique design and perfect 50/50 weight distribution make it a favorite among drivers and drifters. The FD3S model is especially known for its sleek curves and unmistakable rotary sound.


More

Honda NSX (NA1, NA2)

The NSX was Japan’s answer to Ferrari, co-developed with the legendary Ayrton Senna. It combined a mid-engine V6 layout with everyday drivability and Honda reliability. The result was a supercar that could outperform exotic rivals — without the headaches.


More

Tuner Legends & Drift Kings

Nissan Silvia (S13, S14, S15)

The Silvia lineup is famous in the drift world thanks to its RWD layout and SR20DET engine. Agile, lightweight, and easy to modify, the S-chassis cars became fan favorites in street and competitive drifting. The S15 is especially sought after for its modern styling and performance.


More

Toyota AE86 (Sprinter Trueno / Levin)

A lightweight coupe with a high-revving 4A-GE engine, the AE86 became a drift legend — especially after starring in the anime "Initial D". Its simple design and balance made it a grassroots motorsport hero. Even today, it’s one of the most iconic JDM cars ever built.


More

Subaru Impreza WRX STI (GC8, GDB)

Born from rally, the WRX STI blends turbocharged power with AWD grip. The GC8 and GDB models are standout favorites for their raw, aggressive performance. With a big wing and rumbling boxer engine, it’s a road-going rally car with unmistakable attitude.


More

Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution (Evo III–Evo IX)

The Lancer Evo became a cult car thanks to its AWD system, turbocharged engine, and razor-sharp handling. From Evo III to Evo IX, each generation introduced performance upgrades and rally tech. The Evo VI and IX are especially praised for their tuning potential and street presence.


More

Everyday JDM Cars with Cult Followings

Honda Civic Type R (EK9, EP3, FD2, etc.)

The Type R badge transformed the Civic from economy car to track-ready machine. High-revving VTEC engines, tight suspension, and weight-saving design made it a performance gem. The EK9, sold only in Japan, kicked off the Type R legacy in style.


More

Toyota Chaser (JZX100)

Don’t let its sedan body fool you — the Chaser hides a powerful 1JZ turbo inline-6 under the hood. Built for comfort but capable of serious performance, it’s a favorite among drifters and sleepers alike. It blends practicality with raw power in a uniquely JDM way.


More

Mazda Miata / Eunos Roadster (NA)

The JDM version of the Miata, sold as the Eunos Roadster, is a tiny, lightweight convertible designed purely for fun. With RWD, perfect balance, and a spirited engine, it’s beloved by drivers everywhere. The NA generation’s pop-up headlights give it timeless charm.
